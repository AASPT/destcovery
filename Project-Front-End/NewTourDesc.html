Pune

Pune is one of the large metropolises of India situated at the confluence of Mula and Mutha rivers. The city is believed to have existed since the 847 AD. Today the city is known for its educational facilities, IT industry and automotive companies. It is also known as the cultural capital of Maharashtra. Pune has a number of temples, historical sites, museums, zoos and parks. The major attractions in Pune include the circular Nandi Mandapa , Chaturshringi Temple, DagadushethHalwaiGanapati temple, rock-cut Pataleshwar cave temple, Aga Khan Palace,Shaniwarwada, LalMahal, ShindeChhatri and Sinhagad fort, Raja DinkarKelkar Museum, Mahatma Phule Museum, Dr. BabasahebAmbedkar Museum, Kamala Nehru Park, SarasBaug and Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park.


Lonavla & Khandala

Lonavala lies 64 km away from the city of Pune and 96 km away from the city of Mumbai. Khandala lies about 3 km from Lonavala. These twin hill stations in the Western Ghats are popular tourist destinations especially during the monsoon when it turns lush green with waterfalls and ponds. One can experience the enthralling beauty of the Western Ghats at Lonavala and Khandala. Some of the popular tourist spots here are Rajmachi Point, Ryewood Park &ShivajiUdyan, Valvan Dam, Lonavla Lake, Duke's Nose, Tiger's Leap, Karla Caves, Lohagad Fort, Bushy Dam, Shooting Point and Wax Museum.


Mahabaleshwar

Mahabhaleshwar is another beautiful hill station in the Western Ghats. This place is a vast plateau bound by valleys on all its sides and has an average elevation of 1353 m. Mahabhaleshwar is a popular holiday destination and pilgrimage site for Hindus. The Vena Lake, Arthur’s seat, Kate’s point, Needle Hole point and Wilson point are some of the tourist spots where one can enjoy the scenery and natural beauty of the place. The religious sites in Mahabhaleshwarinclude Mahabaleshwar Temple, Panchaganga Temple, Shankar Temple and Krishnabai Temple.
